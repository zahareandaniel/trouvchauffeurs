London Heathrow

London's Premier International Gateway

London Heathrow Airport is the United Kingdom's largest and busiest international airport, handling over 79 million passengers each year across five terminals. Situated 14 miles west of central London in the London Borough of Hillingdon, Heathrow serves as the primary hub for British Airways and a major long-haul gateway for carriers from every continent.

Terminals 2 and 3 handle a broad mix of international carriers including Star Alliance and oneworld airlines. Terminal 4 serves a selection of long-haul and European routes, while Terminal 5 — the largest and most modern of the terminals — is the exclusive home of British Airways and Iberia. Terminal 2, also known as The Queen's Terminal, was comprehensively rebuilt and reopened in 2014 and remains one of the most well-regarded airport terminals in Europe.

For passengers arriving from long-haul destinations, the immigration and baggage collection process at Heathrow can take time. Trouv monitors all flights in real time and your chauffeur will always be in position and ready when you emerge from arrivals — whether your flight lands on schedule, early or late. We include complimentary waiting time on every Heathrow pickup, so there is never any rush.

Our chauffeurs meet all arriving passengers inside the terminal, holding a discreet name board, and are on hand to assist with luggage from the moment you arrive. Whether you are travelling on business or leisure, arriving from a transatlantic red-eye or departing for a short European break, Trouv provides a refined, stress-free transfer experience to and from all Heathrow terminals.
